01 · Overview

Not just a product, a complete development ecosystem.

Octolux solves one of embedded engineering's oldest problems: why does it take six months just to test a new machine? Every time NTX Embedded started a new product, they rebuilt everything from scratch. Custom boards, firmware, UI, and testing tools. The testing process alone routinely took four to six months, burning time and talent on duplication instead of innovation.

I was brought in to design the UX system for Octolux RDS, the Rapid Development System. It bundles pre-validated hardware, firmware, and UI into a single ecosystem, so any PCB or machine is fully testable through the same software. The goal: concept to validated production in 12 weeks.

This was a new concept with no direct precedent inside the company. I led the design from initial research through delivery, interviewing 10 engineers, 3 close collaborators, and 4 senior stakeholders including the CEO and VP of Engineering to validate both the problem and the solution.

05 · The core design decision

Split the system: one side for configuration, one for operation.

The most important decision was not visual, it was architectural. Research showed engineers mentally separated configuration from operation as different modes. So I designed two purpose-built connected systems. This also solved the performance problem: keeping configuration on the desktop keeps the embedded device lightweight and focused entirely on real-time data.

07 · Systems architecture

Five modular layers, each reusable, each composable.

A new industrial product joins the platform by configuring the existing layers, not rebuilding them. This is what enables the 73% development time reduction.

Why modularity changes everything

Traditional development rebuilds all five layers for every product. With Octolux RDS a new product joins by configuring only the top two layers, UI and software, while hardware abstraction, firmware, and hardware stay pre-validated and reusable. That is the architectural source of the 73% reduction: engineers stop rebuilding infrastructure and start building products.

Beyond the numbers

The 73% reduction is the headline, but the deeper impact is that the platform changed what NTX Embedded could attempt. Products that would have needed six-month cycles can now be validated in 12 weeks, opening market opportunities that were not economically viable before.

The QA independence unlocked by the Board Diagnostics View removed a structural bottleneck treated as immovable for years. Manufacturing teams no longer need a firmware engineer present to run validation, a compounding efficiency that improves with every product added to the platform.

How it was built

The production front end was AI-generated. I prototyped the interface with AI-assisted tooling and handed it to engineering as ready-to-use CSS, so the validated design shipped without a separate rebuild step between design and development.
