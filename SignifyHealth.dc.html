01 · Overview

A systems-level trust problem, not a visual one.

Signify was delivering real clinical value: in-home assessments that caught conditions earlier and connected older adults to resources. But members were saying no before they could experience any of it. The problem was not the service, it was the experience leading up to it.

Trust, transparency, communication, and perceived control were all breaking down at different points, and the research had to map all of it before design could touch any of it.

My scope

End-to-end ownership of the research workstream: methodology, user interviews, competitive analysis, AI-assisted synthesis of 300+ behavioral insights, journey mapping across three actor groups, and the final research appendix presented to CVS Health.

The emotional arc

Anxiety peaks at the unknown, not the procedure.

Members were not anxious about the evaluation itself. They were anxious about a stranger entering their home. Clear communication beforehand flattens the spike, long before the clinician arrives.

The unifying insight

Every failure traced to the same root cause: the experience was built to complete a transaction, not to build a relationship. Members were never given the information they needed to feel safe before saying yes.
